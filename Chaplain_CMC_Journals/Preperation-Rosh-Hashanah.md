**Command Journal: The Season of Restoration—Physical, Spiritual, and Tactical**  
**The Mandate**

As Security Forces and Chaplains, we stand at the intersection of physical order and moral welfare. We are mandatory reporters. We see the raw reality of the force—from the systemic complaints regarding the substandard conditions of our barracks to the heavy burden placed on our religious ministry teams who constantly balance the line between spiritual ministers and crisis counselors. We cannot ignore the decay of our physical structures, nor the exhaustion of our people.

Therefore, a new protocol of restoration is required.

**The Physical Rebuilding (Preparing the Ground)**

Starting in May, we are initiating a standing order: every year, with every effort, we will move to repair our buildings and grounds. Every able man and woman will be mobilized to help rebuild the infrastructure of our USMC. This physical labor is not just maintenance; it is a vital preparation for the autumn holy days, culminating in Simchat Torah. We cannot heal a community living in broken structures. The physical grounds must be secured so the spiritual work can begin.

**The Spiritual Truce (Rosh Hashanah to Yom Kippur)**

On September 26, we break from traditional sermons for four weeks to mark the start of Rosh Hashanah. We will end the old calendar and begin a brand new one, looking forward with clear eyes. The repair of the USMC "church" and its people will officially begin with Yom Kippur, the Day of Atonement. On this day, we focus entirely on the internal architecture of our Marines. We repair our service members through repentance, acknowledging our shortcomings, shedding the burdens of the past year, and realigning our moral compasses.

**The Synthesis of Sukkot and Al-Mawasim**

As we move into Sukkot, Shemini Atzeret, and finally Simchat Torah, we recognize the deep, ancient parallels that unite human history. We look to the wisdom of the ancient Arabic traditions to guide this four-week break. These weeks align perfectly with the concept of *Al-Ashhur Al-Hurum*—the sacred months of truce (Dhu al-Qa'dah, Dhu al-Hijjah, and Muharram).

In the ancient world, these months commanded an absolute suspension of all tribal warfare and internal feuding. We will adopt this. For four weeks, internal conflicts, administrative warfare, and unit feuding will cease.

Just as Sukkot is a festival of harvest and temporary dwellings, it perfectly mirrors *Al-Mawasim*, the great pre-Islamic autumn trade fairs like Suq Ukaz. Because our physical grounds have already been repaired, we will use these four sacred weeks of truce to repair our societal ties:

1. **Settling Disputes:** Like the ancient tribes, we will use this ceasefire to settle grievances and repair ALL ongoing business deals and negotiations.  
2. **Community and Trade:** We will engage in the exchange of ideas and resources, ensuring our units are socially and tactically aligned.  
3. **Rebuilding the Mind:** We will hold massive poetic, literary, and intellectual competitions to sharpen the minds of our Marines, replacing the drumbeat of conflict with the rhythm of culture and strategic thought.

**Conclusion: The Cycle Restarts**  
By the time we reach Simchat Torah, the cycle is complete. The buildings are repaired, the souls are atoned for, the disputes are settled, and the truce has healed our divisions. We roll the scrolls back to the beginning. We start the new calendar not as a fractured unit fighting the elements and each other, but as a fully restored force—ready for whatever the wild jungle of the world brings next.
