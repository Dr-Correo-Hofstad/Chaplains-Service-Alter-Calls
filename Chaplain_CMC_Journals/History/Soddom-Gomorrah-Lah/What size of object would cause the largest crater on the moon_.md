An object between **170 and 400 kilometers (105 to 250 miles) in diameter** caused the largest crater on the Moon. \[1, 2\]

The largest impact feature on the Moon is the ancient **South Pole–Aitken (SPA) basin**. Located on the lunar far side, this massive scar measures roughly **2,500 kilometers (1,550 miles) wide** and over 8 kilometers (5 miles) deep. \[3, 4\]

## **The Scale of the Impact**

Because the basin was formed roughly 4.3 billion years ago, scientists rely on advanced computer modeling to estimate the size of the original space rock: \[5, 6, 7, 8\]

* **Glancing Blow Models:** Recent 3D impact simulations suggest that if the asteroid struck at an angle (30° to 45°), it was likely a differentiated body spanning **260 to 400 kilometers** across. For perspective, an asteroid of this scale is roughly the size of Saturn's moon Mimas.  
* **Direct Impact Models:** Simpler models assuming a vertical, head-on impact estimate the projectile could have been smaller, around **170 to 200 kilometers** wide. \[1, 2, 5, 9\]

## **The Buried Remnants**

The collision was so intensely violent that a massive anomaly of dense material—likely the heavy **nickel and iron metallic core** of the impacting asteroid—remains buried hundreds of kilometers beneath the crater floor today. This hidden mass is estimated to be roughly five times larger than the Big Island of Hawaii. \[10, 11\]
